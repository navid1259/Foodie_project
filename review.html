Big Way Hot Pot

Score: 4.5 / 5

Big Way is always a little chaotic due to the popularity of the chain, especially because we visted the downtown location. Though busy, service was timely and the food was prepared quickly and well. We throughly enjoyed our meals and the free ice cream was the cherry on top!

Dan got mostly meat, some vegetables, and vermicelli noodles. Which mixed together with the miso broth was heavenly. Benny and Tyler got all meat, some vegetables, and no noodles due to their diets. This makes Big Way an good option if you are looking to stay within your dietary restrictions.

Danbo Ramen

Score: 4 / 5

Though a bit far from our school, we trekked over to try this little ramen shop. It was a cloudy day and we got there right before the lunch and hail storm began. While in line we recieved a pencil and paper to streamline our order.

The Noodles were al dente and the broth was rich and flavourful. Dan ordered basic noodles, with rich and medium spice and gyoza on the side. Benny ordered basic noodles with a small bowl of rice on the side. Navid and tyler got the basic ramen with refills.

Marugame Udon

Score: 3.5 / 5

A minutes walk away from Costco, a staple destination for our group. Used to be very busy, but now less so. This restaurant offers Udon and Rice dishes, that come with a big selection of tempura.

Dan got the beef udon bowl with the option of no tempura. Benny chose to get the chicken curry again, with an extra chicken tempura. Tyler got beef udon with an extra chicken tempura on the side. Finally, Navid got a beef rich dish with an egg.

No.1 Beef Noodle House

Score: 5 / 5

This location is not close to the downtown BCIT, but the Burnaby BCIT. It is about a 10 minute walk from Burnaby's BCIT. A regular spot we tend to go to when were around. The best thing about this place is its selection of food!

Dan, Tyler and Benny got A7, which is a braised beef noodle dish with beef pieces, tripe, and tendon. Navid as you can see got Taiwanese chicken nuggets and he LOVED it.

Kita no Donburi

Score: 4.2 / 5

Kita No Donburi located very close to our campus. We hiked a whole 2 minutes to get to this location. As this restaurant tends to fill up fast.

Dan got the salmon donburi and the rest of us got katsu curry. This place is known for its "Donburi", which are Japanese style rice bowls

Pholicious

Score: 4.2 / 5

As you can tell, we thoroughly enjoy noodles and found this little gem not too far away from campus. Great service and the workers always try to remember our names when we come in.

We usually all get the pho here because of its afforability and taste.
